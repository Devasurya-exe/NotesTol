HardDisk

* Hard disk is the most common storage device in the computer system.
* It is also known as Harddrive, fixed disk drive etc.
* It is used to store data and program permenant manner ,that is it does not get erased even when the computeris switched off.
* This device is also known as hard disk because it's hard, rigid and made eith aluminium or glass-ceramic materials(disk platter).
* Information is stored in the hard disk by using a maganetic recording method.

Disk Platter

* Most common size - 3 and a half inch
* Portable Computer Platter size - 2 and a half
* Latest small platter - 1 and a half inch

* Available in 10MB or more
* Number of platter can be from 1 to 10 (Common number used is 2 or 3 )
* Made from alluminium alloy for the light weight,But alluminium metal is not thermally stable
* Leading to use glass/glass-ceramic compound.
-more storage,small size
* Material used for platter is called Substrate.

* Two types of coating are ther for coating the disk platter
1.Iron Oxide
2.Thin film media

Iron oxide

* Iron oxide componet is used for the coating.
* 30 micron inch size.
* Not possible for high density storage.
* Crashes can easily occur if scracthes are present.
* Tradition method of coating.

Thin Film Media

* Thickness is 1 to 4 micron inches.
* Very hard and perfect and so can withstand crashes
* Two methods to achieve this coating are:
1.Platting process
2.Sputtering process

Platting Process


* The disk plate is immersed in diffent chemicals

Sputtering Process


* 1st coated with nickel phosphorus
* 2nd 1 to 2 micron inch of cobalt alloy material is deposited
* 3rd Carbon is coated for protection
* This coating helps to survive Head crashes.
* Cost of coating is very high.

Read/Write Head

* Used to read/write information on the disk.
* Each surface contains a head for reading and writing.
* Single unit movement ,in and out.
* Head is close to the disk surface when the harddisk is at rest.
* When the disk rotates on a very high speed air pressure develops between the head and the disk surface.This creates a gap of 3 to 10 micron inches.That is there is no direct contact between the head and the disk surafce. * Rotating speed of disk surface is 3600 to 7200 rpm
* Dust particles can make permenent damage.So harddisk are manufactured in class 100 clean rooms.
* When damaged hardisk are usually replaced than repairing due ti the require ment of a highly clean place to do so.

Types Of Head:

1. Ferrite Head
2. Metal-in-gap Head
3. Thin film Head
4. Magneto-resistive Head

Ferrite Head

* This was used in the original harddisk drive.
* The head is made of Iron-oxide core wrapped with electro-magnetic coils.
* Read or Writing of information is achieved by energizing the coil, That is applying magnetic filed.
* Not suitable for high storage.

Metal-in-gap Head

* Unlike ferrite head high storge is suitable.
* A metal alloy is placed between the recording gap.

Thin-Film Head

* 2 micron inch head is used therefore light weight.
* Photo-lithographic process is used to make this type of head.
* Iron-Nickel alloy core is used.
* 2 to 4 times more magnetically powerfull.
* Costly.
* High density sotrage suitable.

Magneto-Resistive Head

* Most superior head design.
* IBM developed.
* Read/Write is achieved by changing the resistance.
* Separate heads for reading and writing.So proper recording gaps for can be maintained.
* Proper covering is required and electro- static discharges may occur.
